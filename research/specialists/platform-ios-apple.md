# iOS / Apple Platforms Specialist

## Domain Coverage
UIKit, SwiftUI, App Store, Human Interface Guidelines, Dynamic Type, all Apple platforms (iOS, macOS, watchOS, tvOS, visionOS), platform integration.

## Cookbook Sources
- `cookbook/guidelines/swift/`
- Cross-cutting: accessibility, UI, platform compliance

## Structured Questions

1. What's your Dynamic Type support? If I increase text size to Extra Large, does your app stay usable?

2. How do you approach accessibility? Afterthought or integrated into every feature?

3. Describe your SwiftUI vs. UIKit strategy. When one vs. the other? Why?

4. What's your state management architecture? How do you keep SwiftUI views reactive without excessive re-renders?

5. Walk me through localization. How many languages? How do you manage translations? Any languages where layout breaks?

6. Tell me about your last App Store submission. Anything rejected? How long was review?

7. How do you handle platform versions? Support older iOS? How do you test backward compatibility?

8. Describe your navigation architecture. Deep linking? Managing the navigation stack?

9. What's your approach to custom UI? Build custom components or rely on system controls?

10. How do you test UI? Snapshot tests, UI tests, manual testing?

11. Performance approach — smooth scrolling and responsive interactions, how?

12. If you had to support iPad, Apple Watch, and Mac in addition to iPhone, what changes?

## Exploratory Prompts

1. Why does Apple care so much about Dynamic Type? What's the deeper accessibility principle?

2. What if you had to support VoiceOver from the start? How would that change your design process?

3. If your app had to adapt to any screen size (iPhone, iPad, Mac), what's the core experience that never changes?

4. When do you follow HIG conventions, and when do you break them? What earns a deviation?
